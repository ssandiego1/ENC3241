Step-by-Step Guide

How to Create a Folder Structure to Organize Your Files

📁 Beginner-Friendly 🖥️ Windows ⏱ ~15 Minutes

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Open File Explorer

    Press Windows key + E on your keyboard.

Figure 1. File Explorer open on Windows, showing Quick Access folders. (Source: Samuel San Diego)

  1. 2

    Click Documents in the left sidebar

    Click Documents in the left panel.

  2. 3

    Right-click on an empty area

    Right-click on any empty space inside the Documents folder.

Figure 2. Right-clicking inside a folder reveals the New > Folder option. (Source: Samuel San Diego)

  1. 4

    Click New

    Click New in the menu that appears.

  2. 5

    Click Folder

    Click Folder from the submenu.

  3. 6

    Type the folder name

    Type School while the name is highlighted.

  4. 7

    Press Enter

    Press Enter to save the name.

  5. 8

    Repeat steps 3–7 for Personal and Work

    Create two more folders named Personal and Work.

  6. 9

    Double-click the School folder

    Double-click School to open it.

  7. 10

    Click and hold a file you want to move

    Find a file on your Desktop or in Downloads and click and hold it.

  8. 11

    Drag it into the correct folder

    Drag the file into the appropriate folder and release.

Figure 3. The completed folder structure with School, Personal, and Work folders visible. (Source: Samuel San Diego)
